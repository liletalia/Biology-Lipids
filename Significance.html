Biochemical Processes

Biochemical processes are the chemical reactions that serve as the basis of all the cellular processes inside an organism. According to Rollo and Gillaspy (2023), these processes are involved in synthesis of complex molecules from simpler ones, the breakdown of biological molecules into simpler ones, and the alteration of biomolecules to create new molecules. These processes are essential for all living organisms, as they provide the energy and raw materials needed for growth, development, and reproduction. For lipids, these involve lipogenesis, lipolysis, fatty acid oxidation, eicosanoid synthesis, cholesterol metabolism, lipid transport, membrane biogenesis, lipid signaling, lipid remodeling, and lipid peroxidation.

Metabolic Pathways

The term "metabolism" alludes to all of the chemical processes that occur within a cell. In order to maintain its own viability and the health of the entire organism, cells are continually engaged in millions of chemical processes. Frequently, these chemical reactions are connected in chains or pathways (Berg, Tymoczko, & Stryer, 2002). These pathways can also be classified as a “metabolic pathway”. A study done by Baron (2022) insists that for the living thing that is executing them, metabolic pathways are crucial. The energy, resources, and substances required by the body's cells are produced through the metabolic processes. The metabolic pathways incorporate a sequence of processes where the intermediate metabolites are continually renewed and the substrate is continuously reconstructed.

Disruptions and Health Issues

Disruptions in metabolism can lead to various health-related issues such as diabetes, Gaucher’s disease, and hemochromatosis (Kandola, 2023). These metabolic disorders can cause different symptoms and complications within the body, including tiredness, weight loss or gain, and nausea and vomiting.

Current Research/Medical Applications

Lipids are also involved in many clinical researches and experiments and medical applications. Up until recently, lipids were solely associated with energy and structural roles. However, new research has shown that they are also heavily involved in biological processes, prompting the development of a new field of study called lipidomics. Researchers have managed to delve deeper into the functions of lipids and demonstrate how they can aid in the regulation of inflammation and the immune system, neuronal function, or even provide alternatives to disrupt the energy metabolism of tumors, despite the possibility that they are the cause of obesity or cardiovascular diseases (Rodriguez-Alcala, Pimentel, & Vidigal, 2021).
